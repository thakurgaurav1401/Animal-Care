Stray Dog and Cat
Population

Approximately 62 million stray dogs and 9.1 million stray cats are found across India.

Social Public
Visibility

77% of Indians encounter stray dogs weekly, while 68% see stray cats regularly.

Homelessness of
Companion
Animals

Around 85% of companion animals in India are homeless.

Zoonotic Disease
Risk

Stray animals contribute to the spread of zoonotic diseases like rabies, posing significant public health threats.

Population
Control

Methods like trap-neuter-return (TNR) and contraception are used but face resource and infrastructure challenges.

Awareness and
Access

Public awareness about stray animal welfare is growing, but access to consistent veterinary care remains limited.

Our Mission

We are committed to rescuing, rehabilitating, and protecting animals in distress, ensuring they receive the care they deserve. Our efforts include swift emergency rescues, life-saving medical treatment, and long-term rehabilitation to give animals a second chance at life. Beyond immediate aid, we focus on raising public awareness to foster a compassionate society where animals are free from abuse, neglect, and suffering. Through education and outreach, we inspire communities to take responsibility for animal welfare and promote lasting change. Together, we can create a safe, loving environment where all animals thrive and live with dignity and care.

Our Values

Compassion

We believe in showing empathy and care for every animal, recognizing that their lives are as valuable, meaningful, and precious as our own.

Commitment

Our unwavering dedication to animals guides every action, from rescue efforts to advocacy, ensuring their safety, well-being, and compassion.

Community

We understand that lasting change comes from collective effort and work closely with communities to raise awareness and educate on animal rights.

Integrity

We uphold transparency and honesty in all our operations, ensuring that every donation and effort is put to its best use for the welfare of animals.
